Ramen

INGREDIENTS

  • 8 cups chicken broth or vegetable broth.
  • 1 onion, halved.
  • 4 garlic cloves, crushed.
  • 2-inch piece of ginger, sliced.
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce.
  • 2 tablespoons mirin (Japanese sweet rice wine) or dry sherry
  • 1 tablespoon miso paste (optional)

FOR RAMEN TOPPINGS

  • Ramen noodles (fresh or dried)
  • Sliced cooked pork (chashu), chicken, or tofu.
  • Soft-boiled eggs.
  • Sliced green onions (scallions)
  • Nori (seaweed)
  • Bamboo shoots (menma)
  • Corn kernels
  • Bean sprouts
  • Spinach
  • Mushrooms (shiitake, enoki, etc.)
  • Chili oil or sesame oil (optional)

DIRECTIONS

  1. In a large pot, combine chicken broth or vegetable broth, halved onion, crushed garlic cloves, sliced ginger, soy sauce, and mirin.

  2. Bring the broth to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 30-45 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.

  3. Optionally, add miso paste to the broth during the last 5 minutes of cooking for additional flavor (if using).

  4. Cook ramen noodles according to package instructions until al dente. If using fresh noodles, they typically cook in just a few minutes. If using dried noodles, they may take a bit longer.

  5. Drain the cooked noodles and rinse them under cold water to stop the cooking process. Set aside.

  6. Slice cooked pork, chicken, or tofu into thin slices.

  7. Soft-boil eggs: Bring a pot of water to a boil, gently add eggs, and cook for 6-7 minutes for soft-boiled eggs. Transfer eggs to an ice bath to stop cooking, then peel and slice in half.

  8. Prepare other toppings such as sliced green onions, nori, bamboo shoots, corn kernels, bean sprouts, spinach, and mushrooms.

  9. Divide the cooked ramen noodles among serving bowls.

  10. Ladle the hot broth over the noodles, ensuring each bowl gets a generous amount of broth.

  11. Arrange the desired toppings on top of the noodles and broth, such as sliced pork, soft-boiled egg halves, sliced green onions, nori, bamboo shoots, corn kernels, bean sprouts, spinach, and mushrooms.

  12. Optionally, drizzle with chili oil or sesame oil for extra flavor.

  13. Serve the ramen hot, garnished with additional sliced green onions if desired.
