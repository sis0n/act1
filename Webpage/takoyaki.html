Takoyaki

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour.
  • 2 cups dashi (Japanese soup stock)
  • 2 eggs.
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt.
  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar.
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce.
  • 1 tablespoon mirin (Japanese sweet rice wine)
  • 1/2 cup chopped cooked octopus (tako)
  • 1/4 cup chopped green onions.
  • Takoyaki sauce (store-bought or homemade)
  • Japanese mayonnaise.
  • Aonori (dried green seaweed flakes)
  • Katsuobushi (bonito flakes)

DIRECTIONS

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour, dashi, eggs, salt, sugar, soy sauce, and mirin. Whisk until smooth.

  2. Preheat a takoyaki pan over medium heat. Brush each mold with oil to prevent sticking.

  3. Fill each mold about halfway with the batter.

  4. Place a piece of chopped cooked octopus and some chopped green onions into each mold.

  5. Using a takoyaki pick or skewer, continuously rotate and shape the batter around the filling as it cooks.

  6. Keep rotating the takoyaki balls until they become golden brown and crispy on the outside, and cooked through on the inside.

  7. Remove the takoyaki balls from the pan and place them on a plate.

  8. Drizzle takoyaki sauce and Japanese mayonnaise over the takoyaki balls.

  9. Sprinkle with aonori (dried green seaweed flakes) and katsuobushi (bonito flakes).

  10. Serve hot and enjoy immediately.
