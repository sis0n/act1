Sashimi

INGREDIENTS

  • Fresh fish or seafood (common choices include tuna, salmon, yellowtail, mackerel, squid, octopus, shrimp, scallops, etc.)
  • Soy sauce.
  • Wasabi.
  • Pickled ginger.
  • Optional garnishes (e.g., radish sprouts, shiso leaves, microgreens)

DIRECTIONS

  1. Choose high-quality, fresh fish or seafood from a reputable fishmonger or seafood market. Look for fish that has been properly handled and stored, with clear eyes, firm flesh, and a fresh, ocean-like smell.

  2. Thoroughly clean and rinse the fish under cold water to remove any impurities.

  3. Pat the fish dry with paper towels.

  4. Use a sharp knife to slice the fish into thin, uniform slices. Slice against the grain for optimal texture.

  5. Arrange the sliced fish or seafood on a clean serving platter or individual plates.

  6. Optionally, garnish the sashimi with radish sprouts, shiso leaves, or microgreens for added flavor and presentation.

  7. Serve the sashimi with soy sauce, wasabi, and pickled ginger on the side.

  8. Optionally, serve alongside a bowl of steamed rice or sushi rice to complement the sashimi.

  9. Use chopsticks or your fingers to pick up a piece of sashimi.

  10. Dip the sashimi lightly into soy sauce and a small amount of wasabi, if desired.

  11. Enjoy the sashimi with a bite of pickled ginger to cleanse the palate between bites.
