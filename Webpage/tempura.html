Tempura

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour.
  • 1 egg.
  • 1 cup ice-cold water.
  • Pinch of salt.
  • Assorted vegetables (e.g., sweet potatoes, bell peppers, eggplant, zucchini, mushrooms, etc.)
  • Seafood (e.g., shrimp, squid, fish fillets, etc.)
  • Oil for frying (vegetable oil or canola oil)
  • Tempura dipping sauce (tentsuyu) or soy sauce for serving.
  • Optional: grated daikon radish and grated ginger for dipping sauce.

DIRECTIONS

  1. Wash and cut vegetables into bite-sized pieces. Peel and slice root vegetables thinly.

  2. If using seafood, clean and pat dry with paper towels.

  3. In a mixing bowl, lightly beat the egg.

  4. Add ice-cold water to the egg and mix gently.

  5. Gradually add the flour and salt to the egg mixture, stirring gently with chopsticks or a fork until just combined. Do not overmix; lumps are okay.

  6. The batter should have a thin consistency similar to pancake batter. It should be slightly lumpy and not too thick.

  7. Heat the oil in a deep fryer or large pot to 350-375°F (175-190°C). The oil should be hot but not smoking.

  8. Dip the prepared vegetables or seafood into the tempura batter, coating them evenly.

  9. Carefully place the battered ingredients into the hot oil, a few pieces at a time, to avoid overcrowding the fryer.

  10. Fry until the tempura is golden brown and crispy, about 2-3 minutes for vegetables and 3-4 minutes for seafood. Flip the pieces halfway through cooking for even browning.

  11. Use a slotted spoon or wire mesh skimmer to remove the cooked tempura from the oil and drain on paper towels or a wire rack.

  12. Serve the tempura immediately while hot and crispy, with tempura dipping sauce (tentsuyu) or soy sauce for dipping.

  13. Optionally, serve grated daikon radish and grated ginger as condiments for the dipping sauce.
