Onigri

INGREDIENTS

  • 2 cups sushi rice or short-grain Japanese rice
  • 2 1/2 cups water.
  • Fillings of your choice (e.g., umeboshi plum, canned tuna, cooked salmon, pickled vegetables, grilled chicken, etc.)
  • Nori sheets (seaweed sheets), cut into thin strips (optional)
  • Salt.

DIRECTIONS

  1. Rinse the sushi rice or short-grain Japanese rice under cold water until the water runs clear.

  2. In a rice cooker or pot, combine the rinsed rice with water and cook according to the package instructions or until the rice is cooked and fluffy.

  3. Prepare your desired fillings for the onigiri. Common options include umeboshi plum (pitted), canned tuna mixed with mayonnaise, cooked salmon flakes, pickled vegetables, grilled chicken, etc.

  4. Once the rice is cooked, let it cool slightly until it's comfortable to handle.

  5. Moisten your hands with water and sprinkle a little salt on your palms to prevent the rice from sticking.

  6. Take a small handful of cooked rice (about 1/4 cup) and place it in the palm of your hand.

  7. Make an indentation in the center of the rice and place a small amount of your chosen filling into the indentation.

  8. Gently cup your hands around the rice and filling, and press the rice together to enclose the filling and form a triangle, oval, or round shape.

  9. Use your hands to mold and shape the rice into a compact ball or triangle.

  10. If desired, wrap a strip of nori (seaweed) around the outside of the rice ball. This step is optional but adds flavor and texture to the onigiri.

  11. Repeat the shaping process with the remaining rice and filling to make additional onigiri.

  12. Serve the onigiri immediately, or wrap each one individually in plastic wrap for later consumption.
