Udon

INGREDIENTS

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour.
  • 3/4 cup water.
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt.

FOR UDON BROTH (KAKEJIRU)

  • 4 cups dashi (Japanese soup stock)
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce.
  • 2 tablespoons mirin (Japanese sweet rice wine)
  • 1 tablespoon sugar.
  • Optional: sliced green onions (scallions) for garnish.

OPTIONAL UDON TOPPINGS

  • Sliced cooked chicken, beef, pork, or tofu.
  • Cooked shrimp or tempura shrimp.
  • Spinach.
  • Kamaboko (fish cake)
  • Shiitake mushrooms.
  • Nori (seaweed)
  • Poached or soft-boiled egg

DIRECTIONS

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour and salt.

  2. Gradually add water to the flour mixture while stirring with a wooden spoon or chopsticks until a rough dough forms.

  3. Knead the dough on a floured surface for about 5-7 minutes until smooth and elastic.

  4. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and let it rest at room temperature for about 30 minutes.

  5. Divide the rested dough into 4 equal portions.

  6. On a floured surface, roll out one portion of the dough into a thin rectangle, about 1/8 inch thick.

  7. Fold the rectangle into thirds lengthwise, then cut the folded dough crosswise into thin strips to form udon noodles.

  8. Repeat with the remaining portions of dough.

  9. Bring a large pot of water to a boil over high heat.

  10. Add the udon noodles to the boiling water and cook according to package instructions or until the noodles are tender but still chewy, usually about 8-10 minutes.

  11. Stir the noodles occasionally to prevent sticking.

  12. Once cooked, drain the noodles and rinse them under cold water to stop the cooking process. Set aside.

  13. Bring the broth to a simmer over medium heat, then reduce the heat and let it simmer gently for 5-10 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.

  14. Taste and adjust seasoning if necessary.

  15. Divide the cooked udon noodles among serving bowls.

  16. Ladle the hot udon broth (kakejiru) over the noodles, ensuring each bowl gets a generous amount of broth.

  17. Add desired toppings such as sliced cooked chicken, beef, pork, tofu, shrimp, spinach, kamaboko, shiitake mushrooms, nori, or a poached or soft-boiled egg.

  18. Garnish with sliced green onions (scallions) if desired.

  19. Serve the udon hot, accompanied by additional soy sauce or toppings if desired.
